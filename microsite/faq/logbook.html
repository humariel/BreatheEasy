Log Book

In this section we'll detail our progress throughout the semester, stating our most important decisions.

Week 1

Team Building and introduction to the course (presentation of some themes, mentors and various others informations).

Week 2

Project distribution. Meeting with mentor about the project and it's goals.

Week 3

First official meeting of the team with mentor and collaborator. In this meeting we defined some of the technologies to use in this project, namely FIWARE, and which was the target audience and problem to be solved. We discussed the structure of the project and started to think how we would distribute work. We also defined the MVP and possible works after MVP completion.

Week 4

Discussion with Course Directors about the project. The course directors did not like the idea of using FIWARE for it would simplify way too much the project. In discussion with our mentor we though of a way to keep it simple so we wouldn't use FIWARE but the project would be scalable. In talks we thought about using Apache Kafka for it's scalability porperties and work around it. By the end of the week we started to gather contacts for a better revision of personas and scenarios.

Week 5

Contacts were made with people of interest (a councilwoman in OAZ municipality and a researcher of DAO-UA) in order to gather more information via interviews. In these interviews we were more clarified about how our system could help our primary and secondary target audience. For the primary target audience, the municipalities prefectures, our system wouldn't just help them monitor the environment but also how a municipality prefecture could uphold EU regulamentations about the environment. Our secondary target audience, the environmental technicians and engineers, our system would be most helpful to evaluate risks and scenarios. The latter case (scenarios evalutaion) would also be extremely helpful for municipalites. With this new information was of great use for us to reevaluate our current scenarios of use and personas.

Week 6

Meeting with the course professors on the first Milestone. An exploration on the technologies to use, specially Apache Kafka and the Node.js kafka interface.

Week 7

The group started to implement the system with eyes on the core of the main functionality : share data from sensors and API's in the form of graphics at a client interface.

Week 8

The group focused on continuing the work from the previous week in order have the system demo with the core of the main functionality working.

Week 9

The group after the presentation focused on preparing MVP documentation.

Week 10

Due to Easter vacation the group decided to make a pause.

Week 11

The group was overloaded with other projects and felt the necessity to continue the pause.

Week 12

The group met with the mentors to discuss the achievable objectives for the next milestone. Analysis of the 'oneM2M' protocol for IoT platforms, based on previous questions from the teachers in Milestone 3. The group decided to design its own IoT platform and discard existing alternatives, since the elements of the group would greatly benefit from its develpment.

Week 13

The group met to discuss the viability of using geojson to delimit municipalities, which would allow the Cityzoom platform to display the real time location of each device. It was decided that this concept would be developed.

Week 14

The group implemented all the previously planned concepts. The group also implemented the possibility for external APIs to send values from their devices. Worked with members of the MobiWise project to have the Cityzoom platform receive data from their sensors.

Week 15

Integrated the Veolia sensor kits in the project.

The Data-layer suffered some significant changes. The group left kafka behind and built a new broker with mqtt, due to the problems and dificulties kafka started presenting.

Week 16

We changed the map. Taking into account the given critics, we changed how the map displays information by making smaller divisions (hexagons). After a meeting with one of our personas we added realized it would be a good idea to show concentration values of the most common/concerning air pollutants. We also added an alert system that notifies people when anything possibly hazardous happens (like the temperature being too high).

Current Global Assessment

3/4/19

For now we feel like we are making solid progress and starting to see the core of the system get shape. We are looking forward to deploy more sensors across the city.

25/5/19

The group has been able to integrate several sources of data in the project, which was a big goal for the group. Furthermore we are focusing on the critics received in the prior presentation and working on improving our map and how the information is presented to the final user(having a concern on the division of the map and scales used).
